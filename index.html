The start of my crafting journey - Purses

I homeschooled my daughter because I wanted her to have the opportunity to be exposed to more things than traditional school offered. One of the things I wanted her to learn to do was sew. While she was in sewing class, I decided that instead of sitting an waiting for her to finish, I would take the class,too. My first project was a purse.

My daughter was exposed to sewing and took away the basics. I on the other had discovered that I had a love for craft sewing. I loved (and still love) the beautiful fabrics and textures. Making purses became a hobby.

A new path in the journey - Beading.

Eventually, I learned many techniques to make my purses unique. One technique is called crazy quilting. I made a cute little bag using that technique and decided that it needed a unique handle to complement the fabics used. I decided on beads.

The beads looked beautiful on the bag, but I realized that I really didn't understand how to secure the beads to make a quality handle, so I took beading classes!

The journey - Today

Beading quickly overtook sewing as my favorite , and very expensive hobby. Fast forward 15 years. My house now looks like a jewelry store and I have enough beads to start my own bead shop. I give jewelry as gifts and donate pieces for charity events. I am a crafter for life!
