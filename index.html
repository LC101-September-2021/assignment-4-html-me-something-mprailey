The start of my crafting journey - Purses

I homeschooled my daughter because I wanted her to have the opportunity to be exposed to more things than traditional school offered. One of the things I wanted her to learn to do was sew. I had this idea in my head that in order to be a "finished" young woman, she should know how to do certain things.

While she was becoming a finished young woman, I was sitting and waiting. It occurred to me that instead of sitting around waiting, I should perfect some of the skills she was learning. So while she was being "finished", I joined some ladies and began sewing weekly. I selected a purse that was on display by the instructor as my first project.

My daughter was exposed to sewing and took away the basics and I could check that off of the list of skills I had included as part of her education. My daughter did not find sewing to be something she wanted to pursue beyond the basics. I on the other had discovered that I had a love for craft sewing. I loved (and still love) how the different colors, fibers, and textures in fabric could be combined to make fun and interesting purses. Making purses became one of my favorite hobbies.

Eventually, I discovered many techniques to make unique purses and handbag. One technique is called crazy quilting 1. I made a cute little bag using that technique and decided that it needed a unique handle to complement the fabics used. I decided on beads.

The beads looked beautiful on the bag, but I realized that I really didn't understand how to secure the beads to make a quality handle, so I had to take beading classes!

A new path in the journey - Jewelry Making.

Beading lead to other Jewelry making techniques. I even took metal working classes. This proved to be even more fun than purse making. It is also and very expensive hobby.

 
 
 

The journey - Today

Fast forward 15 years. My house now looks like a jewelry store and I have enough beads to start my own bead shop. I give jewelry as gifts and donate pieces for charity events. I am a crafter for life!
